THE PROJECT


The Palazzo d'Accursioof Bologna, originally the residence of the jurist Accursio and expanded over the centuries, has long served as the city’s seat of government. Renovated in the 15th century and enriched by papal interventions from the 16th century onward, it preserves important works such as the Madonna with Child by Niccolò dell’Arca and the statue of Pope Gregory XIII. Inside, notable spaces include the Sala Farnese, decorated with significant painting cycles. Today the palace also houses the Civic Art Collections and the Morandi Museum.
