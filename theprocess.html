Identification of the topic


Our idea was to search for information on Palazzo d'Accursio within ArCo and identify any gaps or areas that could be enriched. Before starting the research, we wrote an ASK query to verify the existence of Palazzo d'Accursio in the ArCo database.


PREFIX arco: <https://w3id.org/arco/ontology/arco/>
PREFIX rdfs: <http://www.w3.org/2000/01/rdf-schema#>
ASK
WHERE {
?person rdfs:label "Palazzo d'Accursio".
}


Then, we verified whether a picture of Palazzo d'Accursio was present with an OPTIONAL pattern.

PREFIX rdf: <http://www.w3.org/1999/02/22-rdf-syntax-ns#>
PREFIX rdfs: <http://www.w3.org/2000/01/rdf-schema#>
PREFIX arco: <https://w3id.org/arco/ontology/arco/>
PREFIX a-cd: <https://w3id.org/arco/ontology/context-description/>

OPTIONAL { ?cp foaf:depiction ?depiction }

Extraction of IRI and information


After verifying the existence of Palazzo d’Accursio and examining related images, we formulated a query to retrieve its exact IRI.

PREFIX arco: <https://w3id.org/arco/ontology/arco/>
SELECT DISTINCT ?cp
WHERE {
?cp a arco:ArchitecturalOrLandscapeHeritage ;
rdfs:label ?l .
FILTER(REGEX(?l, "Palazzo d'Accursio", "i"))
}
LIMIT 10


This led us to the IRI of Palazzo d’Accursio: https://w3id.org/arco/resource/ArchitecturalOrLandscapeHeritage/0800262039. From there, we began exploring its ontologies and the various pieces of information associated with it.

COMMENTS ON THE 3 DIFFERENT TRIPLES AND BEHAVIOUR OF LLMS


The first triple, proposed by Chatgpt, states that the building has a distinct physical object (DecorativeApparatus) identified by a URI. It creates a distinct resource for the fresco and linking it via core:hasPart. The second model, stated by Mistral, uses a blank node ([...]) instead of a URI to represent the decorative apparatus. Conceptually, it is very similar to the first case, but it does not assign a persistent identifier to the fresco: we cannot easily reuse or link this resource elsewhere.

The building is directly linked to the type of decorative apparatus (fresco) via a property (hasInternalDecorativeApparatusOrDecorativeCycle). However, this property is not part of the standard ArCo ontology, suggesting a looser interpretation or hallucination.



Faced challenges


Several factors made the execution of this project particularly challenging. First of all, the ArCo SPARQL endpoint often proved unstable: on one occasion it was completely unusable for several hours, and in many cases, even when provided with correct queries, it failed to return results within the given time limit. As a result, we had to wait considerable amounts of time to obtain responses and frequently had to rerun the queries at different times.

A second difficulty lay in identifying suitable elements to propose as additions to Palazzo d’Accursio within ArCo. We realized that the knowledge graph does not include IRIs for many historical figures for artists like Prospero Fontana. This limitation made it impossible for us to suggest triples that incorporated this type of information. Consequently, we chose to focus on more generic elements—such as statues and rooms—for which we were able to find IRIs, and which we then enriched with descriptive labels in the proposed triples.

Large Language Models (LLMs) turned out to be a double-edged sword: on the one hand, they were useful, but on the other, they often proved unstable and unreliable. Some answers concerning precise information were inaccurate, and certain triples they generated contained predicates not present in the ArCo ontology. To address these issues, we manually verified the information using reliable sources and carefully checked the proposed triples against the ArCo ontologies to identify and correct the errors.
