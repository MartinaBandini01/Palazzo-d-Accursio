ABSTRACT OF THE PROJECT


This project explores the intersection of cultural heritage and semantic technologies through the case of Palazzo d'Accursio, a landmark that embodies Bologna’s civic and artistic history.


Our focus is on the palace’s artistic heritage — its halls, decorated rooms, frescoes, and statues — with the aim of enriching the ArCo Knowledge Graph.


Using SPARQL queries and Large Language Models (LLMs), we collected, verified, and expanded historical and artistic information, which was then formalized into new RDF triples.


This website presents the methodology, the findings, and the semantic contributions developed to improve the visibility and accessibility of cultural heritage data in linked open formats.
